The Bogoda Bridge

The Bogoda Bridge, situated near the town of Badulla in Sri Lanka, is an ancient marvel and one of the oldest surviving wooden bridges in the country. Dating back to the 16th century, this iconic bridge stands as a testament to traditional Sinhalese architecture and craftsmanship. Constructed entirely from wood, including massive wooden beams, pillars, and intricate roof structures, the Bogoda Bridge showcases impressive architectural techniques of the era. Spanning the Gallanda Oya stream, this picturesque bridge is adorned with unique carvings and is a remarkable cultural heritage site, drawing visitors keen on history, architecture, and Sri Lanka's rich cultural legacy.
